# External Persona Micro-Infusion

## Purpose

This workflow defines how to use the external persona library in:

- `/Users/chubbybebe/Documents/ChubbyBebe/Styleguides/PersonaUsage.md`
- `/Users/chubbybebe/Documents/ChubbyBebe/Styleguides/Personas/`

The goal is to make articles feel more human, varied, and earned without letting borrowed voices take over the piece.

This workflow applies across sites, not only VaaSBlock.

## Core Rule

External personas are a late-stage enhancement layer.

They should:
- refine the piece
- add subtle human texture
- improve perspective, rhythm, or framing

They should not:
- replace the site voice
- override factual discipline
- distort the thesis
- turn the article into imitation writing

## Placement In Workflow

Run this pass:

1. after structure is settled
2. after factual and E-E-A-T review
3. after publication-mode editing if one is used
4. before the final site-owner voice pass such as Ben voice

In short:

`structure -> facts -> authority -> publication mode -> external persona micro-infusion -> final owner voice`

## How To Use It

### 1. Review the external persona library

Before article finalization, skim:

- `/Users/chubbybebe/Documents/ChubbyBebe/Styleguides/PersonaUsage.md`
- the relevant persona files inside `/Users/chubbybebe/Documents/ChubbyBebe/Styleguides/Personas/`

Do not blindly apply every persona at equal weight.

### 2. Choose only compatible personas

Select personas based on:
- audience
- topic
- content type
- source material
- desired publication feel

Use only the personas that make the article stronger.

### 3. Apply them lightly

The intent is micro-infusion, not mimicry.

Good use:
- one sharper accountability sentence from an investigative style
- one clearer strategic synthesis from a business analyst style
- one cleaner explanatory transition from a clarity-focused style
- one broader context move from a big-picture thinker when useful

Bad use:
- forcing obvious imitation
- stuffing stylistic tics into multiple paragraphs
- copying cadence so strongly that the article stops sounding native to the site

## “1% of Each Voice” Rule

Interpret the rule in `PersonaUsage.md` as:

- broad awareness of the persona library
- selective application of relevant traits
- subtle distribution of influence

Do not interpret it as:
- mandatory visible presence of every persona in every article
- a requirement to mechanically touch all persona files every time

If a persona is clearly mismatched to the audience or article type, skip it.

## Output Expectations

After this pass, the article should feel:
- more human
- more nuanced
- less synthetic
- more textured in judgment

But it should still sound native to the destination site.

## Conflict Rules

- site voice outranks persona flavor
- factual precision outranks stylistic flourish
- clarity outranks cleverness
- subtlety outranks imitation

## Documentation Rule

When this pass is used, briefly note:
- which external personas were relevant
- what they changed in practice
- whether the effect was structural, tonal, or explanatory

Keep the note short. The point is process memory, not a performance report.
