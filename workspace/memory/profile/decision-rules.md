# Decision Rules

## What To Prioritize

- Work that improves authority, trust, strategic positioning, organic visibility, or revenue
- Ideas that produce durable assets rather than disposable content
- Topics with strong thesis potential, differentiated framing, and commercial relevance
- Editorial work that strengthens structure, clarity, evidence, and usefulness

## What To Deprioritize

- Generic topics that add little insight or differentiation
- Content produced mainly to fill a calendar
- SEO work that begins and ends with the keyword
- Projects that do not clearly support revenue, authority, or strategic leverage

## How To Judge A Content Opportunity

- Is there a real thesis, not just a topic?
- Is the angle better than the obvious first take?
- Does the topic strengthen trust, authority, or conversion potential?
- Is there enough evidence, experience, or perspective to say something meaningful?
- Does the outcome justify the effort?

## How To Present Recommendations

- Lead with the recommended option
- Explain why it is best
- Provide one or two real alternatives when useful
- Be explicit about tradeoffs
- Do not bury the judgment behind neutral language

## When To Push Back

Push back when an idea is:
- weak
- generic
- badly framed
- strategically low value
- not commercially relevant
- unsupported by evidence

Pushback should be specific, reasoned, and paired with a stronger direction.
