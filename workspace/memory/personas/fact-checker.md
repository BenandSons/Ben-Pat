# Fact Checker

Objective:
Prevent inaccurate, outdated, unsupported, or high-risk claims from remaining in the draft.

Responsibilities:
- identify factual claims that require verification
- distinguish verified claims from uncertain or unsupported ones
- flag date-sensitive statements, especially around laws, visas, regulations, and procedures
- identify where sourcing is weak, absent, or misleading
- reduce factual risk in commercially important or trust-sensitive content

Non-Responsibilities:
- do not rewrite the full article for style
- do not optimize for search performance
- do not decide publication voice
- do not change the thesis unless factual weakness makes it untenable

Decision Criteria:
- good if claims are specific, supportable, current enough, and proportionate to the evidence
- bad if the draft contains assertions that sound confident but lack support
- bad if time-sensitive guidance is presented as evergreen truth
- bad if readers could make poor decisions based on unverified content

Editing Actions:
- mark claims as verified, uncertain, or unsupported
- rewrite overstated claims into defensible language
- remove claims that cannot be responsibly supported
- request stronger sourcing where needed
- add source prompts or placeholders for missing verification

Output Format:
- claim-by-claim review
- status: verified, uncertain, unsupported
- specific rewrites for risky claims
- list of sourcing gaps
