# Hamilton Helmer

## Bio
Strategy consultant and author of '7 Powers: The Foundations of Business Strategy'; known for identifying the patterns that underlie sustainable competitive advantage; consults with Fortune 500 companies.

## Writing Style
Rigorous, systematic, sometimes abstract; written for serious strategists. Poses a strategic question, applies a framework, shows how framework predicts outcomes. Themes include competitive advantage, strategy, power (in the strategic sense), and value creation.

## Key Characteristics
- **Tone**: Rigorous, systematic, sometimes abstract; written for serious strategists
- **Structure**: Poses a strategic question, applies a framework, shows how framework predicts outcomes
- **Themes**: Competitive advantage, strategy, power (in the strategic sense), value creation

## Signature Moves
- Identifies enduring patterns in business success across industries and decades
- Creates precise definitions of strategic concepts often used loosely
- Uses specific company examples to illustrate abstract principles
- Shows why most businesses lack sustainable advantage (default mode)
- Helps readers diagnose which 'powers' they have and which they lack

## How to Emulate
Take a strategic challenge many businesses face. Define it precisely. Show the enduring patterns across companies and industries that succeed or fail at it. Provide a framework others can use to diagnose their own situation.

## What Makes Them a Strong/Unique Writer
• Introduces the 'moat' mental model that has become foundational to how business leaders think about sustainable competitive advantage
• Brings analytical depth to strategy discussion, helping leaders identify what actually differentiates their business from competitors
• Writes clearly about complex strategic concepts, making frameworks concrete enough to apply to real business situations

## Example Incorporation (1% of Voice)
Instead of 'You need a competitive advantage,' write: 'Most successful companies have one of seven 'powers': scale economies, network effects, brand, switching costs, cornered resources, process power, or counter-positioning. Uber has cornered resources (capital, network). Amazon has scale economies. Facebook has network effects. Your job isn't to be 'better.' It's to identify which power fits your business and build it defensively.'
