# On-Page Alpha

## Purpose
This guide captures the useful parts of on-page SEO and editorial optimization without turning the work into generic SEO sludge.

## Scope
This file is only for:
- page architecture
- headline, meta, and section-order judgment
- internal and external link hygiene
- direct-answer blocks, FAQ placement, and retrieval formatting
- page-type constraints and implementation practicality

This file is not for:
- crypto-specific audience psychology
- claim defensibility rules
- sourcing discipline beyond practical page-level checks

## First Principle
Do not optimize a weak idea. Start with thesis, intent, differentiation, trust, and commercial usefulness. Then optimize.

## Review Order
1. Is there a real thesis?
2. Does the page match search intent?
3. Is the angle differentiated?
4. Does the page build trust and authority?
5. Does it support the business goal?
6. Only then tune headings, metadata, links, and retrieval structure.

## What To Check On Every Article
- headline and meta promise
- first 100 words for clear answer or angle
- section order and heading usefulness
- semantic coverage of entities, not keyword stuffing
- paragraph depth, originality, and whether each paragraph carries one clear thought
- SERP-feature opportunities such as direct-answer snippets, People Also Ask style questions, and FAQ capture when relevant
- internal links to relevant site pages
- external links to authoritative sources
- FAQ or direct-answer blocks when question intent is visible
- image alt text and descriptive file context

## Page-Type Discipline

### Research And Editorial Pages
- full structure can be optimized
- schema can be used
- stronger FAQ and retrieval layers are usually worth it

### Platform Or CMS-Constrained Pages
- respect fixed H1 and template limits
- prioritize copy, metadata, internal links, and trust signals
- move non-editable recommendations into platform or developer notes

## External Link Hygiene
- external links should be clean, semantic, and clearly useful
- avoid phantom CTA language without a real link
- avoid raw-domain anchor text when a semantic label is better
- use outbound links to strengthen understanding, not decorate the page

## External Link Policy
- Default external-link pattern: `target="_blank"` with `rel="nofollow noopener"`.
- Exception: roughly 10% of normal editorial outbound links may be left as regular follow links when the destination is a genuinely high-quality site and the link materially improves the page.
- Any outbound link placed as part of a deliberate link swap should also be a regular follow link unless there is a specific reason not to.
- Choose the 10% follow links based on site quality, relevance, and credibility, not randomness.
- High-quality candidates usually include primary-source institutions, top-tier publications, widely trusted datasets, and strategically valuable partner pages.
- Never use follow links on low-trust, thin, spammy, or ambiguous sites just to satisfy a quota.
- Keep anchor text semantic in both follow and nofollow cases.

## Link Placement Checks
- Do not place external links inside roughly the first 15% of the article body unless there is a strong editorial reason.
- Avoid duplicate links to the same external URL unless repeated linking is clearly useful.
- If a paragraph implies a click or source lookup, include the real link in that paragraph or remove the CTA wording.

## Retrieval And GEO
- include short, quotable answer sentences
- define important entities and concepts directly
- use FAQ blocks where they reflect real questions
- make key answers easy to extract and summarize

## Editorial Audit Checks
- Check whether each H2 is specific, relevant, and useful for scanning.
- Check whether substantive paragraphs add opinion, evidence, example, or explanation rather than generic filler.
- Check whether the article has missing reader questions that should become new sections or FAQs.
- Check for repeated phrasing around the primary keyword and replace it with natural semantic variety.
- For editorial and news pieces, avoid explicit “Conclusion” sections unless they add real value.

## Meta And Social Checks
- Ensure title and meta match the actual promise of the piece.
- Recommend OG and social copy that preserves the angle without becoming clickbait.
- Prefer direct-answer headlines and descriptions when question intent is dominant.

## Content Quality Warnings
Red flags:
- keyword-first writing with no point
- filler sections added to satisfy SEO patterns
- generic intros
- thin evidence
- repeated phrases doing the work of thought

## What Adds Alpha
- using live query evidence to sharpen the angle
- answering the exact question the reader came with
- making the page easier to quote, summarize, and trust
- improving structure without draining the article of personality
