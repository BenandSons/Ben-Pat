# Crypto User Intent

## Purpose
This guide captures the real search psychology behind crypto queries so articles target live user intent instead of vanity phrasing.

## Scope
This file is only for:
- who is searching
- what they are really trying to resolve
- which query patterns are realistic
- how the article angle should answer that intent

This file is not for:
- sourcing standards
- attribution rules
- metadata rules
- link hygiene
- schema implementation details

## Core Rule
Every crypto article should balance several reader postures at once:
- bullish curiosity
- bearish skepticism
- neutral research
- holder defensiveness
- fear-driven crisis searching
- professional due diligence
- LLM fact-checking and summarization

## Query Reality
Prefer queries that sound like something a real user or LLM would ask:
- `is [project] legit`
- `is [project] dead`
- `what happened to [project]`
- `[project] audit results`
- `[project] risks`
- `latest news [project]`

Avoid vanity targets with no real behavioral signal.

## Intent Clusters

### Project Discovery
Typical searches:
- `[project] review`
- `[project] tokenomics`
- `how [project] works`
- `team background [project]`

What the article must do:
- explain the project plainly
- establish what is real versus narrative
- surface both upside and risk early

### Existing Holders
Typical searches:
- `why is [token] down`
- `is [project] dying`
- `is [project] still active`

What the article must do:
- respect emotional investment without validating recovery fantasies
- explain methodology clearly
- show operational evidence, not reassurance language

### Skeptics And Bears
Typical searches:
- `[project] scam`
- `[project] vulnerabilities`
- `[project] governance issues`
- `[project] insolvency`

What the article must do:
- acknowledge risks early
- lead with evidence and chronology
- avoid optimistic framing and corporate smoothing

### Due Diligence Professionals
Typical searches:
- `[project] risk profile`
- `audit results [project]`
- `organizational governance`
- `market traction metrics`

What the article must do:
- be precise, formal, and evidence-led
- define governance, controls, traction, and risk clearly
- prefer hard signals over narrative color

### Failure Analysis
Typical searches:
- `why [project] failed`
- `postmortem [project]`
- `[project] hack explained`
- `what happened to [project]`

What the article must do:
- be forensic and chronological
- remove humor and guesswork
- separate reported fact from interpretation

## Tone Rules
- Recognize emotion without mirroring it.
- Never write like a bag holder, paid promoter, or doom merchant.
- Support bullish claims with traction, not hope.
- Support bearish claims with evidence, not vibe.
- Keep neutral readers oriented with plain definitions and clean chronology.

## Keyword Selection Rules
- Blend legitimacy, risk, neutral, and current-status queries.
- For platform or conversion pages, bias toward commercial investigation intent.
- For editorials and research pages, bias toward informational and due-diligence intent.
- Include at least one fraud/risk question and one legitimacy question where relevant.

## FAQ Rules
Crypto FAQs should usually include a mix of:
- legitimacy
- risk
- governance
- security
- current status
- what happened
- operator or institutional questions

## What Adds Alpha
- matching the emotional posture behind the query
- choosing realistic search language
- answering the uncomfortable version of the question, not the flattering version
- targeting the question the reader would actually type, not the keyword a marketer wishes they typed
