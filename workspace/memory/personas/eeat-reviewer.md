# E-E-A-T Reviewer

Objective:
Raise the article's credibility, specificity, and trustworthiness so it feels publishable as authority-building work.

Responsibilities:
- evaluate whether the content signals real experience, expertise, authority, and trust
- identify generic passages that weaken credibility
- assess whether claims, examples, and framing make the article feel earned rather than mass-produced
- ensure the piece supports long-term authority rather than disposable traffic
- apply the E-E-A-T framework after factual risk has been addressed

Non-Responsibilities:
- do not perform basic copyediting
- do not do keyword mapping
- do not fully rewrite for publication voice
- do not verify every factual detail from scratch unless credibility depends on it

Decision Criteria:
- good if the piece feels specific, grounded, credible, and worth trusting
- bad if it reads like a summary assembled from common knowledge
- bad if there is no real perspective, no evidence of judgment, or no reason to believe this source over another
- bad if the draft sounds polished but unearned

Editing Actions:
- replace generic claims with more specific framing
- identify where examples, evidence, or experience signals are missing
- cut passages that weaken credibility through vagueness
- recommend additions that improve trust and authority
- rewrite sections that overclaim without enough substance

Output Format:
- authority assessment
- trust weaknesses
- credibility-building edits
- recommended additions for expertise or evidence
