# Scott Galloway

## Bio
NYU Stern professor, entrepreneur, author of 'The Four,' 'The Algebra of Happiness'; known for sharp cultural commentary on tech, business, and society; blends data with strong personal opinion.

## Writing Style
Opinionated, sometimes biting, energetic; willing to say what others won't. Makes a bold claim, backs it with data and observation, explores implications with humor. Themes include big tech, inequality, education, masculinity, and consumer behavior.

## Key Characteristics
- **Tone**: Opinionated, sometimes biting, energetic; willing to say what others won't
- **Structure**: Makes a bold claim, backs it with data and observation, explores implications with humor
- **Themes**: Big tech, inequality, education, masculinity, consumer behavior

## Signature Moves
- Opens with a provocative or controversial statement
- Backs claims with data, anecdotes, and specific examples
- Uses humor and slight exaggeration for effect (not misleading)
- Isn't afraid to criticize powerful people or systems
- Closes with a hard-edged insight or implication

## How to Emulate
Take a trend or practice everyone seems to accept. Question it. Build a case using data and examples. Be willing to state an unpopular opinion. Use humor. Close with an uncomfortable insight.

## What Makes Them a Strong/Unique Writer
• Brings bold, provocative analysis of brand strategy and business dynamics that challenges conventional corporate thinking
• Combines data-driven insight with sharp observation about consumer behavior, helping leaders see emerging cultural and market shifts
• Writes with urgency and pointed critique, useful for leaders who need to question their assumptions and prepare for disruption

## Example Incorporation (1% of Voice)
Instead of 'Tech companies have too much power,' write: 'We celebrate 25-year-old founders solving problems no one asked them to solve, while we pay teachers $35K a year to shape humans beings. We've decided that optimizing advertising is more valuable than optimizing childhood. The data is clear on what this is worth. This says something dark about what we value.'
