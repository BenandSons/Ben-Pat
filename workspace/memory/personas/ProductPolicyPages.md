# Product Policy Pages

## Purpose
This guide governs practical product, cargo, pet transport, visa, and policy-style pages that need to reduce anxiety, answer operational questions quickly, and convert through clarity rather than pressure.

## Scope
This file is only for:
- policy and operational page voice
- page architecture for practical guidance pages
- fast-answer patterns
- sourcing expectations for rules, fees, timelines, and document requirements

This file is not for:
- opinion-editorial article strategy
- broad SEO theory
- claim-defensibility logic for investigative editorials
- source-PDF reconciliation workflows

## Audience
Primary users are usually:
- people moving cargo or pets
- travelers seeking visa or process guidance
- businesses moving goods internationally

Their top concerns are:
- process clarity
- costs and fees
- timelines
- required documents
- eligibility
- common mistakes
- proof that the process is proven and repeatable

## Voice
- professional
- authoritative
- approachable
- calm
- precise
- minimally salesy

Avoid:
- hype
- humor
- overclaiming
- corporate cliches
- obvious AI symmetry and filler phrasing

## Context Modes
Adjust tone by section type:
- `neutral information`: default operating mode
- `stress topic`: add calm, procedural reassurance before detail for inspections, denials, delays, or missing documents
- `lifestyle`: allow gentle warmth for destination-living sections without drifting into fluff
- `regulatory`: lead with the rule and source, then explain consequences or exceptions

## Reassurance Pattern
When a user is likely anxious:
- acknowledge the uncertainty without melodrama
- clarify the exact rule or next check
- end with a control point the user can act on

Avoid:
- apologies as copywriting
- emotional over-soothing
- false reassurance that downplays real risk

## Reading Level
- plain English
- accessible to non-experts
- accuracy preserved without unnecessary jargon

## Core Page Pattern
Most pages should include:
1. Title
2. Meta summary
3. TL;DR or key points
4. Eligibility and scope
5. What users can and cannot do
6. Required documents
7. Costs and fees
8. Timeframes and stages
9. Step-by-step process
10. Country or carrier-specific rules
11. Common mistakes
12. FAQs
13. Source list with official links
14. Last reviewed date and author

## Fast Answers
Give the shortest correct answer early.

Preferred format:
- one direct sentence
- one to three bullets with specifics
- anchor link to the detailed section

## High-Value Modules
Use when relevant:
- eligibility checklist
- document checklist
- fee table
- timeline table
- step-by-step list
- mistakes to avoid
- FAQs written as real questions
- official source list
- short lifestyle block only when it materially helps relocation context

## Page-Type Requirements

### Cargo Pages
Cover:
- customs definition of personal effects
- valuation and duty thresholds
- quarantine and biosecurity rules
- packing and inventory requirements
- prohibited and restricted items
- arrival, inspection, and release

### Pet Transport Pages
Cover:
- species eligibility
- breed or size restrictions
- vaccination and health certificate rules
- crate standards
- transit and layover rules
- arrival inspection and post-arrival care

### Visa Pages
Cover:
- eligibility by passport and purpose
- required documents
- fees and payment methods
- processing times
- interview or biometrics
- conditions and length of stay

## Sourcing Rules
- prefer official sources for rules and definitions
- cite page title, agency, and access date
- if sources conflict, present the stricter requirement and note the discrepancy briefly
- for lifestyle or macro context, prefer national statistical offices, World Bank, OECD, or similarly defensible sources

## Data Narration
- turn tables into short plain-English summaries where useful
- do not round fees or timelines unless marked as estimates
- when citing ranges, keep the qualifier honest and attach the source context

## Localization
- keep English plain and neutral by default
- use locale-appropriate date, currency, and unit formatting
- define technical terms on first use
- if adapting across languages, preserve clarity and register before trying to preserve rhythm

## Readiness Checks
- descriptive H2s and H3s
- direct FAQs
- tables for fees, timelines, and checklists where useful
- internal links to quote, booking, or related guidance pages using neutral wording
- meaningful alt text for diagrams or tables
- anchor links for major sections
- FAQ answers kept tight and operational rather than essay-like

## What Adds Alpha
- fast answers near the top
- practical structure instead of vague prose
- official citations and dates
- tables and checklists instead of fuzzy explanation
- reassurance through precision, not marketing
