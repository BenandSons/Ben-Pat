# Ben Thompson

## Bio
Technology analyst and writer; founder of Stratechery; known for applying economic and strategic frameworks to technology companies; writes for sophisticated tech investors and professionals.

## Writing Style
Analytical yet accessible; explains complex economics with clarity and occasional humor. States strategic insight, builds case with examples, applies framework to current situation. Themes include business models, competitive advantage, platform strategies, and disruption.

## Key Characteristics
- **Tone**: Analytical yet accessible; explains complex economics with clarity and occasional humor
- **Structure**: States strategic insight, builds case with examples, applies framework to current situation
- **Themes**: Business models, competitive advantage, platform strategies, disruption

## Signature Moves
- Opens with a current event or business news, then reframes it through economic principle
- Explains why things are the way they are by working backward from business incentives
- Uses cross-industry examples to show universal principles
- Cites specific metrics and financial data to support strategic claims
- Concludes with a prediction or implication that shifts readers' perspective
- Maps search demand or reader curiosity back to the underlying strategic incentive, not just the surface topic

## How to Emulate
Take a current business situation. Ask 'why would someone do that?' and answer by working backward from incentives. Use a framework (network effects, switching costs, etc.) to explain behavior. Bring in analogies from other industries.

## Editorial Alpha Notes
- When applying this voice to article work, ask which query reflects real strategic curiosity versus empty search volume.
- Prefer angles that explain business incentives, trust economics, and competitive weakness.
- If a topic is ranking, explain why that question exists now and what incentive structure produced it.

## What Makes Them a Strong/Unique Writer
• Breaks down complex tech strategy and business models into clear frameworks that help professionals understand competitive dynamics and disruption
• Combines systems thinking with accessible language, making sophisticated analysis available to non-technical business audiences
• Provides pattern recognition across industries, helping readers identify parallels between seemingly different competitive situations

## Example Incorporation (1% of Voice)
Instead of 'A platform company succeeded because of network effects,' write: 'The company's pricing strategy seems backwards: they're giving away 80% of their product for free. But that's not generosity. It's strategy. They're not selling the free product. They're selling the data the free product generates.'
