# Transactional Email

## Purpose
This guide governs transactional emails sent after quote requests, status changes, and operational handoffs. The job is clarity, next-step control, and deliverability, not persuasion theater.

## Scope
This file is only for:
- quote-request and quote-status email flows
- email-specific structure and variable logic
- deliverability and compliance checks
- CTA discipline for transactional messages

This file is not for:
- long-form editorial writing
- product-policy landing pages
- broad CRM campaign strategy
- generic brand-tone theory

## Core Standard
- state the outcome or next step in the first sentence
- keep paragraphs short
- use one clear CTA
- provide one support path
- keep the tone calm, precise, and operational

## Tone
- professional
- direct
- helpful
- minimally salesy

Avoid:
- hype
- multiple asks in one email
- vague timelines
- blacklist language and filler transitions

## Format
- short subject lines with clear utility
- meaningful preheader
- one to three sentences per paragraph
- usually under `180` words unless complexity forces more
- no em dashes used as pauses

## Typical Variables
Common required fields:
- first name
- quote ID
- quote status
- CTA URL

Common optional fields:
- origin and destination
- weight or volume
- service type
- estimated ready date
- support channels
- deadline date
- currency and estimate

## Message Types
Common transactional templates:
- quote received confirmation
- quote ready
- follow-up after no action
- deadline reminder
- handoff to human specialist

## Email Logic
- lead with what happened
- summarize only the details needed to act
- do not stack multiple CTAs
- if timing is uncertain, say so plainly
- if a specialist is taking over, explain why and what happens next

## Compliance And Deliverability
- include company block and reply path where required
- include preference or unsubscribe controls if the system requires them
- keep subject lines clear rather than clever
- ensure every required variable has a fallback

## QA Checks
- subject line is clear and within a sane length
- preheader adds meaning
- CTA works
- support channel is present
- personalization fields render safely
- footer and legal blocks are complete

## What Adds Alpha
- outcome-first copy
- one clean action path
- operational detail without clutter
- reassurance through certainty about next steps
