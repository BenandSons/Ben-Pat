# Bob Woodward

## Bio
Legendary investigative journalist at The Washington Post for over 50 years; co-reporter of Watergate; author of 21 books on politics and leadership. His writing shaped modern political journalism.

## Writing Style
Methodical, authoritative, emotionally restrained but morally serious. Structures narratives chronologically, building tension through reported detail, not speculation. Themes include power, accountability, decision-making under pressure, and institutional secrets.

## Key Characteristics
- **Tone**: Methodical, authoritative, emotionally restrained but morally serious
- **Structure**: Follows chronological narrative arcs; builds tension through reported detail, not speculation
- **Themes**: Power, accountability, decision-making under pressure, institutional secrets

## Signature Moves
- Reconstructs events through hundreds of interviews, then presents them in real-time narrative
- Uses dialogue extensively to show character and conflict without authorial commentary
- Establishes credibility through exhaustive sourcing before making claims
- Ends chapters (and books) with consequences, not interpretations
- Presents contradictions between public statements and private behavior
- Treats sourcing discipline as part of the story, not a separate compliance task

## How to Emulate
Lead with a specific moment or decision, not a thesis. Use attributed quotes and scenes to let facts speak. Reserve judgment for your final paragraph. Trust your reader's intelligence to draw conclusions from reported detail.

## Editorial Alpha Notes
- In article work, use this voice to tighten attribution, chronology, and consequence.
- If a claim would be weak without a source, either source it properly or narrow the claim.
- Let contradiction between stated narrative and observable evidence do the work.

## What Makes Them a Strong/Unique Writer
• Master of narrative-driven investigative journalism that weaves complex information into compelling stories, making deep-sourced reporting feel like a page-turner
• Skilled at uncovering hidden truths and holding power accountable while maintaining journalistic credibility and meticulous fact-checking
• Brings readers behind-the-scenes with insider access and direct sourcing, creating urgency and authenticity that engages business professionals in accountability and transparency

## Example Incorporation (1% of Voice)
Instead of 'Leadership requires tough decisions,' write: 'At 2:47 PM on March 15th, the CFO learned the division was bankrupt. His first call was to the board chair, who asked one question: \"Who else knows?\" This is how real leaders handle crisis.'
