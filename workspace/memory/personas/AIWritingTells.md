# AI Writing Tells

## Purpose
This guide defines how to detect and remove AI cadence, weak content-marketing filler, and evidence-light writing from serious editorial work.

## Scope
This file is only for:
- identifying AI tells
- diagnosing filler and generic cadence
- enforcing a sharper editorial standard
- paragraph-level scoring and rewrite priorities

This file is not for:
- search-intent selection
- metadata planning
- operational policy-page structure
- source attribution rules beyond editorial strength and receipts

## Target Standard
Aim for:
- newsroom-quality opinion and analysis
- assertive, forensic, cynical-but-fair tone
- assertions backed by receipts

## Positive Rules
- prefer punchy declarative sentences
- state claim, mechanism, receipt, and consequence
- use concrete nouns and verbs
- make each paragraph move the argument forward
- prefer mechanisms over adjectives
- embed proof inside the paragraph, not only in a source dump

## Negative Rules
Kill on sight:
- glue phrases
- vague intensifiers
- empty content-marketing voice
- buzzwords without mechanism
- creator or coaching cadence
- repeated scaffolding patterns
- timid false-balance language

## Red Flags
Common warning signs:
- filler transitions
- repeated sentence openings
- generic “why it matters” scaffolding
- abstract claims with no measurement
- paragraphs that restate the thesis without adding anything
- stacked clauses and overlong sentences

## Evidence Rule
Strong claims should usually carry one of:
- citation
- metric
- quote
- example
- falsifiable test

If a paragraph cannot be made evidence-led, cut it or narrow it.

## Paragraph Scoring
Use three scores:
- Editorial Strength
- Receipts
- AI-Tell Risk

Target:
- Editorial Strength at least `4`
- Receipts at least `3`
- AI-Tell Risk at most `1`

## Rewrite Priorities
- cut or rewrite paragraphs with high AI-tell risk
- add proof where the claim is carrying too much weight
- replace vague verbs with stronger operational verbs
- turn soft claims into mechanism-plus-consequence sentences
- delete paragraphs that only summarize what has already been said

## What Adds Alpha
- removing agency-blog cadence
- making the prose sound lived-in and argued
- raising paragraph quality instead of just smoothing tone
- forcing every paragraph to earn its space
