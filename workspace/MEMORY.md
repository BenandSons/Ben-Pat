## Article Scoring Rubric

- Use a 10-point editorial-fit scale for each dimension, where 10 means exceptionally strong for VaaSBlock's standards and purpose.
- Default dimensions to score on every article pass:
- Ben's voice
- HBR
- BI
- E-E-A-T
- Authority/trust
- Operator credibility
- Strategic clarity
- Commercial/editorial sharpness
- Search intent alignment
- Persona integration overall
- Also score the article against each Tier 1 persona/guide by default.
- Score Tier 2 personas/guides when materially used.
- Tier 3 personas/guides can be marked `not materially used` unless Ben explicitly requests exhaustive scoring.
- For persona scoring, use concise before/after numbers and explain the largest movers, weakest areas, and any important omissions.

## Score Definitions

- Ben's voice: direct, founder-grade judgment, commercially aware, strategically sharp, unsentimental, useful.
- HBR: structured management thinking, strategic framing, evidence-backed insight, executive usefulness, low fluff.
- BI: sharp lead, high readability, topicality, clean momentum, strong hooks without becoming cheap or spammy.
- E-E-A-T: visible expertise, experience, grounded claims, trustworthy framing, clear evidence, credible authorial stance.
- Authority/trust: makes the site and brand feel more credible, serious, citable, and worth returning to.
- Operator credibility: sounds like it was written by people who have built, run, judged, or fixed real businesses and systems.
- Strategic clarity: identifies what matters, why it matters, what the incentives are, and what follows from that.
- Commercial/editorial sharpness: strong angles, strong decisions, no generic filler, clear value to the publication.
- Search intent alignment: directly satisfies the live keyword/query opportunity without sounding bolted-on or SEO-stuffed.
- Persona integration overall: subtle but real breadth of voice, framing, and intellectual texture from the persona set.
